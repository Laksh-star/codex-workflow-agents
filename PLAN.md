# Product Strategy: Top 5 Codex-First Non-Coding Workflows

## Summary
Position Codex as an **operator for business workflows**, not just a chatbot. The strongest wedge is work that combines:
- live organizational context from connectors,
- proactive follow-through through automations,
- UI-level reach through `computer-use`,
- and **editable deliverables** through native `Excel` and `PowerPoint`.

The first 5 use cases to prioritize are:
1. Personal Chief of Staff
2. Executive Briefing Machine
3. Daily Leadership Digest
4. Deal Desk Assistant
5. Customer Voice Aggregator

## Prioritized Use Cases
### 1. Personal Chief of Staff
Best buyer: solo founders, executives, staff roles.

Feature bundle:
- Slack/Teams triage and reply drafting
- reminder and follow-up automations
- meeting-to-actions extraction
- `computer-use` for tools without connectors
- optional digest output in doc/deck form

Why build first:
- high frequency problem
- clear daily value
- low adoption friction
- strong “agentic” story because it can monitor, draft, remind, and operate tools

Positioning line:
- “Codex acts like an ops-minded chief of staff that tracks commitments, drafts responses, and follows through across your actual tools.”

### 2. Executive Briefing Machine
Best buyer: founders, business leads, chiefs of staff.

Feature bundle:
- pull from Slack, Teams, GitHub, local files, dashboards
- generate editable `Excel` KPI pack
- generate editable `PowerPoint` briefing deck
- scheduled weekly/monthly automation
- `computer-use` to collect data from dashboard UIs when APIs are missing

Why build first:
- output is tangible and high value
- leverages the most differentiated artifact capabilities
- easy to demo with visible before/after

Positioning line:
- “Codex turns scattered operating signals into a board-ready packet you can still edit in Excel and PowerPoint.”

### 3. Daily Leadership Digest
Best buyer: managers, directors, VPs.

Feature bundle:
- Slack/Teams/GitHub signal collection
- summary of blockers, risks, decisions, and escalations
- recurring daily automation
- optional owner/action tracker in `Excel`
- outbound draft for leadership channels

Why build first:
- broad audience
- recurring workflow fits automation well
- strong cross-source synthesis story

Positioning line:
- “Codex gives leaders a clean daily view of what changed, what is blocked, and what needs escalation.”

### 4. Deal Desk Assistant
Best buyer: sales leaders, customer success, revenue ops.

Feature bundle:
- account-thread synthesis from Slack/Teams
- QBR and renewal-risk packs in `PowerPoint`
- account health trackers in `Excel`
- recurring monitoring for named accounts
- `computer-use` for CRM/admin portals without a connector

Why build first:
- high monetary impact
- direct link to revenue retention and expansion
- `computer-use` is a genuine differentiator here

Positioning line:
- “Codex assembles live account context into customer-ready and exec-ready materials without waiting for integrations.”

### 5. Customer Voice Aggregator
Best buyer: product, support, GTM leadership.

Feature bundle:
- ingest feedback from chats, notes, docs, and web research
- cluster themes and quantify frequency
- output theme tracker in `Excel`
- output product review deck in `PowerPoint`
- recurring trend snapshots

Why build first:
- cross-functional value
- useful even with partial system coverage
- shows Codex can convert unstructured chatter into structured decisions

Positioning line:
- “Codex turns scattered customer signals into prioritized product insight, with evidence you can present.”

## Differentiating Features to Map Explicitly
### Core platform differentiators
- **Native `Excel` skill**: real formulas, charts, filters, dashboards
- **Native `PowerPoint` skill**: editable decks, tables, charts, structured slides
- **Connectors**: Slack, Teams, GitHub, local files
- **Automations**: scheduled recurring runs and thread heartbeats
- **`computer-use`**: access to UI-only workflows and non-integrated systems
- **`image_gen`**: stronger visuals for decks, training, and briefings

### Positioning against normal AI assistants
- normal assistants summarize text
- Codex can **collect, operate, monitor, and deliver**
- the wedge is not “better chat”; it is **end-to-end workflow execution with editable business artifacts**

## Launch and Packaging
### Recommended initial packaging
- Tier 1: Chief of Staff + Daily Digest
- Tier 2: Executive Briefing Machine
- Tier 3: Deal Desk / Customer Voice vertical packs

### Demo sequence
1. show Codex collecting cross-tool context
2. show `computer-use` handling one tool without an API
3. show an automation scheduled to rerun
4. show final deliverables in editable `Excel` and `PowerPoint`

## Success Criteria
- user saves at least 30-60 minutes per recurring workflow
- output is immediately usable without major cleanup
- the workflow works even when one source system lacks a connector
- the demo clearly shows “more than summarization”

## Assumptions and Defaults
- prioritize non-coding buyers over engineering-first use cases
- emphasize recurring workflows over one-off prompts
- treat `computer-use` as a strategic bridge for missing integrations, not the primary UX for every task
- use editable office artifacts as the primary proof of value, since they differentiate this setup most clearly
