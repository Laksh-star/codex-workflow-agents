# Positioning

## Core thesis

Codex should be positioned as an operator for business workflows. The strongest wedge is work that combines:

- live context from collaboration and repository connectors
- proactive follow-through through automations
- UI-level task completion through `computer-use`
- editable outputs through native `Excel` and `PowerPoint`

## Why this is different

Most assistants stop at summarization. Codex can:

- collect context from connected systems
- operate tools that lack clean integrations
- schedule follow-up and recurring monitoring
- produce business-native deliverables that teams can edit and reuse

## Differentiation map

| Capability | What it unlocks | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| `Excel` | KPI packs, trackers, analysis workbooks | output is usable by operators and finance teams |
| `PowerPoint` | board decks, QBRs, leadership briefings | output is presentation-ready and editable |
| Slack, Teams, GitHub connectors | live work context | removes manual copy-paste and stale inputs |
| Automations | recurring briefs, reminders, monitoring | moves from one-shot answers to operational follow-through |
| `computer-use` | UI-only systems, dashboards, admin tools | expands coverage beyond available APIs |
| `image_gen` | polished training or briefing visuals | improves communication quality without custom design work |

## Positioning lines

- Personal Chief of Staff: Codex acts like an ops-minded chief of staff that tracks commitments, drafts responses, and follows through across your actual tools.
- Executive Briefing Machine: Codex turns scattered operating signals into a board-ready packet you can still edit in Excel and PowerPoint.
- Daily Leadership Digest: Codex gives leaders a clean daily view of what changed, what is blocked, and what needs escalation.
- Deal Desk Assistant: Codex assembles live account context into customer-ready and exec-ready materials without waiting for integrations.
- Customer Voice Aggregator: Codex turns scattered customer signals into prioritized product insight, with evidence you can present.

## Launch guidance

- Lead with Tier 1 workflows because they are high-frequency and easy to prove.
- Show `computer-use` only where it solves a real integration gap.
- End every demo with an editable workbook or deck to reinforce the product wedge.
